Strawberry Cheesecake Cookies

photo from: https://www.identicalrecipes.com/dessert-recipes/strawberry-cheesecake-cookies

Description

This cookie is recipe is adapted from this strawberry cookie recipe by Sam Merrit and this strawberry cheesecake recipe by Livia Benson and modified to be eggless

A flavorful strawberry cookie with a cheesecake center!

Ingredients

  • 2.5 c. freeze dried strawberries
  • 1 c. butter
  • 1/4 c. packed brown sugar
  • 1 c. granulated white sugar
  • 1/4 c. buttermilk
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 c. of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp of salt (optional if using salted butter)

Cream Cheese Filling

  • 1 c. cream cheese
  • 2/3 c. powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Topping

  • ~1 c. granulated white sugar (for coating)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to convection 350Â° F
  2. Mix cream cheese with sugar and vanilla with stand or hand mixer whisk. Once well combined, scoop cream cheese in 1 tbsp balls onto a parchment paper lined tray/plate. Place This in the freexer for 30 minutes to an hour. (Should come out to about 12 cream cheese balls)
  3. Put freeze dried strawberries into a food processor to turn into a powder.
  4. To start cookie dough, beat room temperature butter in stand mixer/by hand for 3-5 minutes (or until butter turns off-white).
  5. Add in brown sugar and white sugar to your butter. Beat the sugar with the butter until the mixture is fluffy (about 2 minutes).
  6. Add buttermilk and stir into mixture. Mix in vanilla extract, strawberry powder (and red/pink food coloring if desired).
  7. Add flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt to form your dough. If using a stand mixer, mix on low. If mixing by hand, use a spatula.
  8. If your cream cheese balls are frozen well, flatten 1/3 cup cookie dough in your palm and place 1 cream cheese ball in the center. Fold the cookie dough around until it is well secured in the center. Pour sugar into a separate bowl and roll cookie dough in.
  9. Bake cookies for 10-11 minutes (for 1/3 cup scoop).
  10. For perfectly circular cookies, use a circular bowl/cup/cookie cutter and swirl around your fresh cookie to make edges clean. Then let cookies sit on tray for about 10 minutes before removing.
  11. You can eat these fresh and/or put these in the fridge to store up to 10 days!
